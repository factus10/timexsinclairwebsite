Computers

Welcome to TimexSinclair.com! This site documents the presence of fantastic little home computers from Timex Computer Corporation and Sinclair Research in the United States.

You can find information about the computers and almost all the hardware, software, books, magazines, newsletters, user groups for them and more.

Timex/Sinclair Computers

The Timex Computer Corporation, a subsidiary of Timex Corporation, was formed to market Timex/Sinclair products in the United States. They produced the following computers.

Timex/Sinclair 2068
Timex Computer Corporation's third and last home computer for the United States market was launched in November 1983. Timex attempted to improve on the ZX Spectrum with TS 2068 by adding new features, including sound and high resolution graphics.
Read more
Timex/Sinclair 1500
Announced May 17, 1983, the 1500 was a redesigned 1000 with 16K RAM built in and the case and keyboard of the Sinclair ZX Spectrum. Timex also designed a cartridge accessory (the 1510) and produced a few cartridges to help the 1500 compete against video game systems and other computers with cartridge systems.
Read more
Timex/Sinclair 1000
The Timex/Sinclair 1000, produced under license from Sinclair, was a modestly improved version of the Sinclair ZX81, with 2K RAM. The TS1000 was launched in July 1982 and sparked a massive surge of interest. In the five months following the TS1000's launch, the company sold 550,000 machines.
Read more

Sinclair Computers

Sinclair Research was the most recognizable of a series of names for a company Clive Sinclair started to produce his many inventions. Sinclair Research designed, marketed and sold the MK-14, ZX80, ZX81, ZX Spectrum, QL and a variety of other products. The Z88 was produced by Cambridge Computers, formed after Sinclair sold the name to Amstrad.

Sinclair ZX80
Launched in the United States in June 1980, the ZX80 was notable for its size, weight and price. At 7" x 9", under a pound and at $199.50, it was the smallest, lightest and least expensive home computer available.
Read more
Sinclair ZX81
The Sinclair ZX81, developed by Sinclair Research and manufactured by Timex Corporation in Dundee, Scotland, was the first home computer sold in the United States for under $150.
Read more
Sinclair QL
The Sinclair QL (for Quantum Leap) was launched by Sinclair Research in 1984, as an upper-end counterpart to the Sinclair ZX Spectrum.
Read more
Cambridge Z88
The Cambridge Computer Z88 is a small, lightweight, portable Z80-based computer with a built-in combined word processing/spreadsheet/database application called PipeDream.
Read more

Other Computers

Comp Shop, a British company, sold the MicroAce by mail order in the United States. It was a quasi-licensed clone. Other ZX81 clones, of varying compatibility, were produced around the world.

MicroAce
The MicroAce was a clone of the ZX80 marketed in the US as a kit, under license from Sinclair.

Lambda 8300
The Lambda 8300, which was widely available in the States as the PC8300, was a clone of the Sinclair ZX81.
